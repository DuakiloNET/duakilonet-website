Every developer blog starts the same way: a blank page, a cursor blinking, and the quiet pressure of writing something worth reading.

This is mine.

## Why This Blog Exists

I've been writing notes for years — scattered across Notion, random `.md` files, and the occasional comment in code that was definitely not meant to explain anything. What I *haven't* done is put those thoughts somewhere permanent. Somewhere others could find, read, and maybe find useful.

That changes now.

`duakilo.net` is my corner of the internet. No brand. No content strategy. Just a place to think out loud about things I'm building, learning, and occasionally breaking.

## What I'll Write About

Mostly tech and coding. Some of it will be practical:

- Things I figured out after too much Googling
- Tools and setups that actually improved my workflow
- Short tutorials on things I wish had better documentation

Some of it will be more reflective — how I think about problems, decisions I've made on projects, lessons from things that didn't go as planned.

No fluff. No "10 tips to boost productivity" listicles.

## A Note on Consistency

I'm not going to promise a posting schedule I can't keep. What I will commit to: writing when I have something worth saying.

Quality over cadence.
